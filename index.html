The Signals Are Converging

Amy Webb's CIPHER Framework Applied to the Future of FinOps by Brad Payne

According to my Amazon account, on January 17th, 2020 Amy Webb's book, The Signals Are Talking, shipped to me. I wouldn't finish it for another two years, predicting the future seemed less important three months later in the spring of 2020. One wonders what I might have done differently had I ordered the book a year before.

According to her Amazon profile, Amy Webb is the founder and CEO of the Future Today Institute, a leading foresight and strategy firm that helps leaders and their organizations prepare for complex futures. Amy pioneered a data-driven, technology-led foresight methodology that is now used within hundreds of organizations globally. She is a professor of strategic foresight at the NYU Stern School of Business.

I cannot say I remember when I finished the book, between its pages still are a Las Vegas boarding pass and a postcard from a favorite Los Angeles restaurant. I do remember I was left with the impression that I did not know sufficiently enough about any topic to follow the CIPHER methodology Amy pioneered.

I'm not certain that has changed... but I am certain the signals are converging.

Answering the questions of the cleverly acronymed CIPHER identifies early signals of change before they become mainstream trends. If you'll indulge me, here are the signals I'm receiving, some strong and some faint.

The Convergent Signal

The FinOps Foundation defines the practice as maximizing the business value of technology. The recently updated mission statement isn't a signal that this shift is emerging, it's confirmation that it will arrive. As a FinOps Lead in an organization frantically adopting AI, I'm observing that FinOps is approaching the era where this definition needs to become our reality. FinOps teams that remain focused entirely on cost reduction will see their capabilities decomposed into agentic tools absorbed by adjacent functions. The teams that survive will do so with strong cultural roots across functions and lean into value intelligence.

Our path forward is not uncharted. The FinOps Framework and maturity model provide the structure for how teams navigate this transition. I believe FinOps Leads need to apply system thinking skills to connect costs to business outcomes across their organizations. Growing the capability to frame cloud costs not as line items but as expressions of value is the clearest signal for where FinOps teams must head.
